On the practice

Elin's paintings begin in silence. A long look at a window. The pause between two pieces of music. The space a room holds after someone has left it.

Working primarily in oil, she builds her canvases in thin, patient layers — letting each wash settle before the next, so that colour seems to rise from within rather than sit on the surface. The work resists narrative. It asks, instead, to be sat with.

Marchetti studied Fine Art at Edinburgh College of Art (MFA, 2014) and has exhibited at Ingleby Gallery, the Royal Scottish Academy, and in group shows across Oslo, Copenhagen, and Lisbon. Her work is held in private collections in the UK, Norway, and the United States.

She lives and works in a converted printworks in Leith.
